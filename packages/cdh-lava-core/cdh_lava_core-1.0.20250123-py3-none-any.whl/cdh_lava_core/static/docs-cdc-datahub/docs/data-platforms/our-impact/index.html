CDC Data Hub Impact

As the CDC Data Hub grows, so does our ability to advance programmatic efforts, support public health action, and help the agency move towards a modernized data infrastructure. Track our progress here and see users' stories that show CDC Data Hub in Action.

CDC Data Hub by the Numbers​

  • Users across 13 CIOs
  • Petabytes of cloud data
  • Multiple data assets
  • CDC-authored publications using CDC Data Hub data assets (since 2009)
  • 50+ training attendees in 2023

CDC Data Hub in Action​

See what we're doing and who we're working with to bring the latest innovations in data science to CDC.

CDC Data Hub Partners with Research Networks to Enable Queries of External Data​

In addition to offering direct access to data, CDC Data Hub facilitates CDC's engagement with research networks, such as PCORnet, the National Patient-Centered Clinical Research Network (PCORnet).

[Read more]

Informing Public Health Practice: American Hospital Association Data​

CDC scientists often use CDC Data Hub data and related resources to inform program direction and public health practice.

[Read more]

Data Hub Innovations Support COVID-19​

During the COVID-19 pandemic, a critical question for CDC was: how do we secure timely, big data to support COVID-19 surveillance and research? The CDC Data Hub partnered with the COVID-19 Data, Analytics, and Visualization Task Force and the Public Health Informatics Office to answer this question.

[Read more]

CDC and NIH Partner to Advance COVID-19 Research​

In August 2020, the CDC Data Hub began coordinating COVID-19 research efforts with the NIH Clinical Center. Partners met biweekly to share ongoing and planned projects, learn about each other's work and expertise, and identify areas for collaboration. Discover how this partnership answered critical and timely COVID-19 research questions.

[Read more]

Data Lake Brings Big Data and Custom Data Provisioning to CDC Data Hub​

To address data transport challenges at CDC, the COVID-19 Response's Technology and Innovation Team developed and launched the Data Lake. Housed by the CDC Data Hub, the Data Lake quickly moves data of any size and type and provides a repeatable and scalable business model for a cloud-based big data portal at CDC.

[Read more]

CDC WONDER: New Provisional Mortality Data​

CDC WONDER, the online data access platform, now includes provisional mortality data. Provisional mortality data are non-final, preliminary estimates based on the current flow of death certificate data to the National Vital Statistics System in the National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS). This is an exciting and innovative move to help address existing gaps in data dissemination.
